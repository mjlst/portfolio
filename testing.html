Website Revamp

As a UX designer working on a project to separate the existing identity monitoring service as a new product, I was working on revamping the complete flow of the product. Starting with auditing the existing flow, I provided wireframes and design deliverables for the new pages.

After auditing the exsiting user flow of identitysmart.com, the flow had several recurring loops without indicating users which states they were in or informing why we require certain steps when enrolling. Some of the sections and pages were copied from previous product that weren’t necessary for the targeted users. I listed task list and sorted them out based on importance and difficulty.

Focused Areas

1Checkout Flow

Checkout flow required the system to not only gather payment information but also to increase the completed activation rate.

2Member Area Dashboard

Previously the member area dashboard only provided numbers of alerts and users could not access alert information for the first thing. Also some of the contents were separated in different pages unorganized. Category sorting was the first approach to solve this problem.

3Landing Page

One of the most important separation that had to be made from the previous product was checkout and activation flow. For this flow to be completed and to start the service coverage for the users, we needed to gather customer’s name, date of birth, address and social security number. First flow I suggested was below, combined activation and checkout flow.

Combined activation and checkout flow

Based on the importance of starting service coverage as soon as the user submits the payment method, my first suggestion was to require users to input information that is needed for service activation before the user submits the payment method. However, this flow could easily overburden users with a number of inputs.

Separated activation and checkout flow

Based on the A/B testing conducted, we decided to deliver the design with the separate activation flow. Here, it was proven that when users were provided with small sections of inputs, the activation rate was doubled. Also, it was important to clearly indicate the progress of the activation flow.
