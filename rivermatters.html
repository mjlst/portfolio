Rivermatters, marketing website for a museum

RiverMatters is physical and digital city planning game that markets upcoming exhibition, H2Oh! Carnegie Science Center. RiverMatters enables students and visitors of Carnegie Science Center to explore and understand different needs of city departments and consequences of their action.

Mission

Carnegie Science Center was looking for an experience which can market their upcoming exhibition H2Oh! for their main target demographic, Middle School students, and their general Museum visitors.

Interview Takeaways

Technology barrier
During the interview with middle school teachers we found out that it is not always possible to bring newest technology inside the classroom."

Time Constraint
The class usually takes around 40 to 50 minutes including roll calls, instructions and lessons learned. Therefore, we the experience had to be within 30 minutes."

Educational Standard
The contents needed to be built under educational standards to be introduced to the students in the classroom. There were several educational standards that were suggested from Carnegie Science Center as well."

User Testing

We were conducting 5 different user testing sessions with 24 users. First two user testing was more for understanding user interests. Last three sessions were for testing out prototypes usability.

Side project: physical board game

"In the Classroom"

Based on the found-outs from the interview, we decided to design a physical game that generates a space where students can actively discuss and express their own idea. The game consists of four different departments which will be taken by each student in a team. Then students are asked to come up with the best scoring city plan to compete with other teams. Students need to find out which department is affecting which to reduce negative points and increase positive points.

Main project: marketing website

Web version was integrated with competition application that we created for Carnegie Science Center Staffs. As users are joining the game and score certain points, the score is stored in database. This generates the scoreboard on the website. Also, the map history is stored to create different types of map for each competition.

"At Home"

For the Carnegie Science Center to target their general visitors of the museum, the web accessibility was essential. The solution for the team was to create a similar game based on the board game, yet can be played through the web. Different from the classroom environment, the digital game enables the users to explore and plan out the city individually.
