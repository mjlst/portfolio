How students can utilize more of the school library resources?

In contrast to an increasing number of library resources available, most of the students were not aware of the resources available. To reduce the student's search for their required resources from the school and to increase the library resource usage, I designed a system that utilizes the school online portal and a library management system.

Features

For library system to actively help students to find correlated items that matches their majors and their course registrations, I came up with three key features below.

Checkout History

Users can see the list of items that they checked out with the returning date. Also they can see the details of the book with the time and the library that they visited.

Recommendations

Users will receive recommendations of items based on their search keywords, course registration and their major.

Search Inbox

Search Inbox helps users to find the book by providing maps and decimal codes.

Framer Prototyping

While creating a prototype, it was important for me to provide a value proposition of recommended items. By simply tapping the title of the item, students can read the brief and can decide whether to rent it out. Also, students can see where to find the item and when it is available by tapping the search tool icon on the bottom of each item listed.

Final deliverable

After going through 3 different iterations, I landed on this final high fidelity mockup as below. Every iteration, I removed unnecessary icons and made a variation with different font color to increase attention. In addition, I provided 1/3 of space to show the book cover, after discovering the way students make decisions on books.

Checkout History

This acts as a 'shopping' list for when they are going through the check.

Recommendations

When a section has been completed, it will be grayed out, but the user can still click on it.

Ping the book

Users can skip the item to bring this at the end of the list.

For the search inbox, I increased the size of the image to 80% so that students can easily locate the item by looking for items that matches. Also, I increased the readability of available items by differentiation the colors between available and unavailable items, as well as applying different font color for the availability.

Search Inbox

Students can add items on any lists to their Search Inbox. By sliding through the image and map area, users can see the location of the item with the library map, the indoor map and Dewey Decimal Code. After they check out the item from the library, the item will be removed from the list automatically.

Territory mapping

Students were aware of library locations and been to the library a few times, but never rented any resources. They usually go to the library to study. When they are looking for resources, they often get lost, not knowing what they need or want. Also, it was important for the librarian to market their resources and increase the library usage. The main approach to them was through e-mail marketings and events hosted in the library. However, these events only worked for a short amount of time. They needed stronger connection with students.

Gap Analysis

There were several library management system that was available online, however, students had to know which book that they want, and only could check whether the book is available in a nearby library.

"Many of successful library management systems are mainly focusing on checking in and out with universal management implementation. Several online book clubs have been run based on manual inputs from the users end."
