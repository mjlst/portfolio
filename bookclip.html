Bookclilp, mobile library management system

Bookclip is an application that helps students to actively utilize the library by tracking their book checkout history. Also, it gives item recommendations based on their major and search keywords. This information will be connected to the library management system for further understanding and management for librarians.

"Searching for the right book is not a simple task."

Research Planning

The project was started by having interviews with students about their library usage habit. Brainstorming, creating wireframes, prototyping and testing were included in the process. I conducted interviews, affinity diagramming and user testing for research.

Territory mapping

Persona

Julia | First year college student
Julia wants to make the most out of her new school resources. She has a few experiences of using the school library. She feels like visiting the library and searching for books usually takes more time than she expected.

Robert | Active library user, student
Robert is an active reader. He visits the school library at least once a week. He is also a member of an online book club and enthusiastic about sharing book reviews with his friends. He wants to have an easier way to track the books that he have read.

Gap Analysis

"Many of successful library management systems are mainly focusing on checking in and out with universal management implementation. Several online book clubs have been run based on manual inputs from the users end."

Features

Checkout History

Users can see the list of items that they checked out with the returning date. Also they can see the details of the book with the time and the library that they visited.

Recommendations

Users will receive recommendations of items based on their search keywords, checkout history and their major.

Search Inbox

Search Inbox helps users to find the book by providing maps and decimal codes.

Search Inbox

Users can add items on any lists to their Search Inbox. By sliding through the image and map area, users can see the location of the item with the library map, the indoor map and Dewey Decimal Code. After they check out the item from the library, the item will be removed from the list automatically.
